Our Findings

These insights come from real student experiences.

This study is based on a survey of 52 undergraduate students at Khalifa University. Students answered multiple-choice and rating questions about how they chose their major and how their expectations compared to their actual experience.

Methodology

52 KU students, one institution-wide survey.

The technical report used a quantitative Mentimeter survey with 52 undergraduate students at Khalifa University. The survey included multiple-choice and five-point Likert-scale questions, and the results were analyzed in Excel.

What the study measured

  • Why students chose their major
  • How high their initial expectations were
  • Whether academic reality felt different from those expectations
  • Whether that mismatch created doubt about the major or future career

Interpretation

Students are optimistic, but not fully prepared.

Students generally remain positive about their future, but many enter their major without fully understanding the workload and academic demands. This gap between expectation and reality is where a service like Major Lens becomes useful.

What students actually need

  • Clearer major-specific information before enrollment
  • Early exposure to academic demands
  • More direct advising and faculty contact before students commit
  • Plain-language summaries instead of technical descriptions

Helping students choose a major, or understand the one they already chose, with more clarity and earlier guidance.
